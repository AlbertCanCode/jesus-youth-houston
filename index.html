About the Jubilee Cross

The tradition traces back to 1984, when Pope John Paul II entrusted a large wooden cross to the young people of Rome at the close of the Holy Year of the Redemption, asking them to carry it across the world as a sign of Christ's love. That cross — known ever since as the Youth Cross, or Pilgrim Cross — has since traveled with World Youth Day to cities on every continent.

Jesus Youth is a Catholic charismatic youth movement that began in Kerala, India in the 1970s and took its name in 1985; today it's active in more than 35 countries and was formally recognized by the Vatican in 2016. For the current Jubilee Year, our Houston chapter is carrying a Jubilee Cross from home to home each night — continuing, on a small and local scale, the same pilgrimage first entrusted to young people four decades ago.
